What is ASTRO?

ASTRO is a broadly scoped series of contract vehicles encompassing virtually all services supporting or involving manned, unmanned, or optionally manned platforms and robotics. ASTRO is administered by the General Services Administration’s (GSA) Office of Assisted Acquisition Service (AAS) FEDSIM Organization.

ASTRO is a family of ten separate, individual, Multiple Award (MA), Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ) contracts sponsored by the Department of Defense (DoD).

ASTRO At A Glance

  • Who can order? GSA FAS AAS Contracting Officers.
  • When is the ordering period? ASTRO task orders must be solicited and awarded prior to ASTRO expiration date of November 14th, 2031, assuming option extensions, and task orders may extend up to five years after the ASTRO Ordering period expires.
  • What contract types are supported? All contract types can be utilized at the task order level, including cost reimbursement and hybrid models.
  • Is there a ceiling? No.
  • What labor categories are supported? Standardized labor categories are included and you can create customized labor categories at the task order level when necessary.
  • Is ancillary support allowed? Yes, Other Direct Costs (ODCs) at the task order level are allowed on as needed basis.
  • What service types are covered? Commercial, Non-commercial, classified and non-classified, CONUS and OCONUS.
  • How many companies are on the ASTRO vehicle? There are 129 unique companies split into 10 pools ranging from 25-46 awardees in each pool.
  • How many small businesses are on the ASTRO vehicle? 37 awards were made to small businesses.
  • Is Service Contract Act (SCA) support allowed? Yes.
  • How do I know which pools to use? In general, pools that contain the preponderance of the work should first be researched when considering an ASTRO solution.

ASTRO Pools

ASTRO services are organized into ten functional Pools, with the appropriate Pool selected for task order solicitation based upon the preponderance of work in the requirement being solicited.
To see vendors associated with each pool, select a pool icon.

Access to ASTRO is available through Assisted Acquisition specifically through GSA’s Office of Assisted Acquisition Services (AAS) FEDSIM organization and any GSA AAS Contracting Officer (CO) granted a Delegation of Procurement Authority (DPA).
