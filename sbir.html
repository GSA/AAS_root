SBIR

Overview of Program

GSA AAS has partnered with the U.S. Small Business Administration to launch a pilot program to award and manage Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase III contracts on behalf of the Department of Defense. As a result of this multi-agency partnership, federal agencies will have increased access to cutting-edge technology and small businesses will meet their goals.

SBIR is a competitive program that encourages small businesses to engage in federal research and development that has the potential for commercialization. The program stimulates technological innovation, partners with small businesses to meet federal R&D needs, and increases private-sector commercialization of federal R&D outcomes to increase competition, productivity, and economic growth.

GSA AAS’ pilot program will support acquisitions under Phase III of the SBIR program, which paves the way for new technologies to enter both Government and private sector marketplaces. Phase III work includes one or more of the following: products, production, services or R/R&D (excluding weapons systems).

During SBIR Phases I and II, technical merit, feasibility, and commercial potential of R&D activities are determined. The objective of Phase III is for small businesses to pursue commercialization of the objectives resulting from Phase I/II R/R&D activities. All Phase III R&D or production contracts for products, processes, or services intended for use by the U.S. Government or the private sector must be supported with non-SBIR funds. According to the SBIR statute and the SBA Policy Directive, no further competition is required in Phase III, as competition requirements are met in Phases I and II. Phase III work must be awarded to the successful Phase I/II vendor to the maximum extent practicable.

The pilot program to support Phase III contracts will:

  • Encourage Real-World Applications for R&D: SBIR Phase III addresses the President’s Management Agenda (PMA) Cross Agency Priority (CAP) goal to “improve the transfer of federally-funded technology from the laboratory to the marketplace.”
  • Provide Federal Support for Small Businesses: Contracts under SBIR Phase III help small businesses thrive by bringing new technologies to public and private markets.

FAQs

What is SBIR?

 
SBIR is a competitive program that encourages domestic businesses to participate in federal research and development.

What is SBIR Phase III?

 
Phase III allows the business community to pursue commercialization objectives resulting from Phase I/II R/R&D activities. All Phase III R&D or production contracts for products, processes, or services intended for use by the U.S. Government or the private sector must be supported with non-SBIR funds.

What is the role of GSA AAS?

 
In partnership with the Small Business Administration, GSA AAS is piloting an effort to assist in contract award and contract management of SBIR Phase III contracts on behalf of SBIR agencies. GSA AAS will provide support out of two client support centers initially (Region 5 & Central Office), and expand as needed.

Can GSA AAS assist with SBIR Phase I and Phase II awards?

 
GSA’s SBIR pilot is focused on SBIR Phase III (commercialization) at this time.

For more information on the pilot program, please see the GSA press release.
For more information on the SBIR program, please visit https://www.sbir.gov/about/about-sbir.
