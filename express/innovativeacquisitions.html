Pilot Program for Innovative Commercial Items

 

The General Services Administration (GSA), has established a pilot program to competitively procure innovative commercial items, technologies, and services using the commercial solutions opening (CSO) procedure. GSA has developed this pilot program to be implemented outside the normal Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) requirements to engage traditional and non-traditional Government contractors. The goal of this pilot program is to provide a streamlined approach for acquiring innovative products and services.

 

Our Process

 

We solicit commercial solutions that address current needs of Agency clients (i.e., our DoD partners).

 
 

When there is an open area of interest you can respond to compete for the opportunity by submiting a brief about your solution.

What are the typical projects FEDSIM supports?

 
Traditionally, FEDSIM focuses on complex acquisitions. Our acquisition and program management staff has a deep understanding of IT and professional services that allows us to deliver high-quality solutions.

Will you provide/assign a contracting officer's representative (COR) if one is not available at the requesting agency?

 
Yes. FEDSIM will provide/assign a COR as part of our service.

Will you write the SOW/SOO/PWS for the requesting agency?

 
FEDSIM’s acquisition manager and project manager will work with the requesting agency to write the SOW/SOO/PWS as part of our service.

Is the requesting agency responsible for providing a technical evaluation team?

 
Yes. The requesting agency is responsible for designating the technical evaluation team.

Is there a fee associated with Assisted Acquisition Services?

 
Yes. The assisted acquisition fee is based on each individual acquisition.

Where can I find more information about the Defense Procurement and Acquisition Policy?

 
For more information regarding Defense Procurement and Acquisition Policy, please go to http://www.acq.osd.mil/dpap/. (Opens in a new window)
