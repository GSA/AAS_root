Frequently Asked Questions

What is EXPRESS?

 
EXPRESS is a pilot program offered by GSAâ€™s Office of Assisted Acquisition Services (AAS). EXPRESS provides federal agencies with contract and financial management services that streamline acquisition delivery. We leverage acquisition expertise, tools and templates, and the EXPRESS automated platform, Symphony, to increase efficiency, reduce cost, and support industry innovation through use of non-traditional authorities.

What are the initial steps to begin utilizing EXPRESS services?

 
To begin utilizing EXPRESS services, provide a waiver (DoD only), Interagency Agreement (IA), and Military Interdepartmental Purchase Request (MIPR) or funding document to your EXPRESS point of contact. They will guide you through submitting the requirements package detailed below.

What is a waiver?

 
Along with the Interagency Agreement (IA), the waiver establishes the formal agreement between EXPRESS and DoD customer agencies. Each DoD agency has specific requirements for a signed document in which a designated official expressly approves the use of an external assisted acquisition service such as EXPRESS. Each customer must verify the applicability and unique requirements within the waiver with authorized approvals within their organization.

What is an Interagency Agreement (IA)?

 
The IA establishes the formal agreement between GSA and customer agencies. IA Part A includes the terms and conditions between the two parties and the roles and responsibilities of each party. The IA Part B is the vehicle through which funds from a MIPR or funding document are exchanged. Individuals with signature authority from EXPRESS and the Requiring Agency are required to sign IA Parts A and B.
 

Is my program right for EXPRESS?

 

Supported Contract Types

Firm-Fixed Price, Time-and-Material, Labor Hour

 
 

Max Dollar Threshold

No maximum dollar value on requirements awarded by EXPRESS

 
 

Intelligence Community (IC) Work

EXPRESS will soon provide support for Intelligence Community requirements.

 
 

Other Transaction Authority (OTA)/Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO)

GSA does not have Other Transaction Authority (OTA). Beginning in summer 2018, EXPRESS will be able to utilize a Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) solicitation methodology to acquire innovative commercial solutions.

 
 

Level of Support

EXPRESS provides an Integrated Project Team (IPT), including a Contracting Officer (CO), Contracts Specialist (CS), one Acquisition and Project Manager (PM), and a Financial Manager for each requirement.

Clients must provide a Contracting Officer's Representative (COR). EXPRESS does not provide a COR in order for the requiring agency to maintain full visibility and ownership of post-award execution.

 
 

Acquisition Timeline

75-120 working days after receipt of a complete requirements package.

Timeline dependent on acquisition strategy, dollar threshold, and client responsiveness and feedback.

 
 

Fee

Total assisted acquisition fee: 3.5% of total obligation, including option years. Only 3.5% of the estimated base year costs are required to release solicitation and award the contract.

 
 

Requirements Package

Signed IA Part A
Signed IA Part B
Military Interdepartmental Purchase Request (MIPR) or funding document
Draft Statement of Work (SOW)
Independent Government Cost Estimate (IGCE)
Waiver (DoD clients only)
Market research
Justification documents that detail the precise needs and intended outcomes of the acquisition
If applicable, historical documentation (e.g., previous contracts)

 
 

How do I contact a member of the EXPRESS team for additional information?

Please send an email to express@gsa.gov and a member of the EXPRESS team will respond to your message within two business days. You may also visit our website: https://aas.gsa.gov/express/.
